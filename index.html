About

Research at the boundary of data, institutions, and public decision-making.

I study how machine learning, administrative data, and analytic infrastructures shape decisions in public institutions. My work is grounded in customs and excise problems, but the broader question is how technical evidence becomes trustworthy, accountable, and usable in policy settings.

I am especially interested in cases where models do not merely predict outcomes, but enter workflows: fraud targeting, tariff classification, trade policy analysis, and fiscal instruments for environmental governance.
